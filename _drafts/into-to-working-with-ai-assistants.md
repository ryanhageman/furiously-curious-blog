AI Assistants are everywhere. Chat with them like you do a person and they'll help you get things done. They know a LOT. A LOT LOT. Given how much they know, sometimes it can be a challenge to exactly the thing you're looking for. After all, with all that information to sift through, there can be a lot of right answers to a simple question, depending on what specifically you're talking about.

For example, answer this. 

The sky is?

well?

Did you say blue? 

That's probably the most likely answer. Feeling a bit snarky? Green? Falling? Overcast?

Well, snarky pants. If a tornado is coming, sometimes the sky is? Yeah, green. Just read Chicken Little to someone for the 37th time this week, you probably said falling. 

## Context

Getting the best answer from an AI Assistant is all about context. This thing was probably trained on the contents of the entire public internet, a ton of books, articles, newspapers, images, and who knows what else. It know a lot about a LOT. It needs to know what you're talking about. 

This is what all the "prompt" stuff is setting up. "Prompt Engineering" is a fancy term for giving the AI Assistant the context it needs to return the best answer possible. It really should know things like the subject you're talking about, how you want it to answer, what you actually want it to do, and quite a few other things to return the best thing.

Some things get worked out slowly, over time. The way you want to talk with it. Do you prefer long or short answers? Do you want it to sound like you're talking with Snoop Dogg when you talk about cooking? Do you want it to ask you lots of questions all at once or one at a time? The assistant needs to know these things, for every chat.

Some of the mainstream assistants have a settings section where you can save these "preferences" but there's only so much room there, and what do you add there anyway?

## Ask An AI Assistant

Guess who knows the best way to program an AI Assistant. The AI Assistant! You don't need to know how to set all that stuff up, you can ask the assistant directly. Open a chat and tell it that you want to set up the preferences for how you collaborate. Talk back and forth with it and let it know that you're building instructions for it to work with you. The assistant will talk with you about it and put it all together. 

Once you've had the chat, ask it to give you the instructions, click to copy, that you can paste into the settings section. It'll give you a bunch of text, you click to copy it, and paste it in the settings section. Your AI Assistant is now customized for you. 

I find myself tweaking those instructions pretty consistently, but the main thing is, I don't write them myself, I ask the AI Assistant what would be best, it writes them, and I paste the results in the settings section. Every time it's a new custom upgrade.
